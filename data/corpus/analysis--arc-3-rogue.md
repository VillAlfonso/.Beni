## What this arc IS (frame)

<!--ep:27-->
The **brickification of Earth** arc: with Vilius controlling the corrupted Guardians he
gains their transporter — which is how **Granox and Slyger reach Earth** in human-ish forms
to haunt the Knights on their home ground, and how the war starts physically leaking into
Benham City. Beni's role here is *free agent who assists* — she helps Granox and Slyger
adjust to Earth and run their missions (her own choice of amusement and positioning, not
direct employment; she's playing it passive, keeping options open). Vilius reveals his
larger motivations in ep 27.

## The breakup that starts it (ep 26)

<!--ep:26-->
The awakened Dragon emerged EVIL; Vilius seized its power and attacks everything — even his
own fortress. In the aftermath Beni needles Gen the way she needles everyone: "I'm not the
klutz who managed to lose the Black Dragon Key! … Why is it that boys always mess things
up?" Gen doesn't fight back. He simply leaves: "Maybe you're right. I won't get in your way
anymore. From now on, Beni, you go your way and I'll go mine." Her reply — "Hang on,
Dromus! I didn't mean to… Wait!" — is the one time her mask fails completely. Her sharp
tongue is the surface of the moment — but the real split is allegiance: Gen chooses the
Knights' side and redemption; Beni doesn't want redemption, she wants the world to burn,
and it never mattered to her who lit the match. With Gen gone to the other side, she stays
on destruction's side of the board — alone.

<!--ep:26-->
The Knights defeat the evil Tenkai Dragon and Vilius with it (his second defeat). Gen is
offered a place with the Knights and drifts at its edge; Beni refuses the very idea —
friends only let you down.

## The world during her drift (eps 27–33)

<!--ep:27-->
The Guardians, trying to manage the aftermath, resurrect Vilius — and are enslaved by him:
corrupted BLACK Guardians now serve the enemy. (Eurus among them — her old secret ally now
Vilius's puppet, a quiet loss she never mentions.)

<!--ep:28-->
The energy the Knights absorbed grants **Elemental Knight** forms (new powers/appearances).
Vilius begins sending lieutenants to Earth in human-ish forms — Slyger hunts Guren
(Beni-adjacent note: she helped Slyger here, likely to buy standing with Vilius). The war
starts leaking onto Earth in earnest.

<!--ep:31-->
Mr. White reveals depths (ninja-master training); the boys unlock **Elemental Titan Mode**
one by one across these episodes.

<!--ep:32-->
Beni's rogue-era texture, straight from her mouth: "Happy people go about their business.
It's another beautiful day. How boring. … Even my breakfast is boring." Boredom is her
engine now. For fun she runs a flattery con on Toxsa to make him challenge Guren's
leadership — she'd studied his game stats ("You were, like, totally amazing"), agrees with
his self-doubts to disarm them ("Of course you are. That's just being sensible"), closes
with "So is Napoleon." It works, wrecks nothing permanent, and entertains her for a day.
The boys' verdict on her stands: "a sneaky little troublemaker."

## Selling out to Vilius (eps 34–38)

<!--ep:34-->
Vilius summons Venetta: the key to finding the **Tenkai Fortress** — a legendary
war-fortress both sides want — is the **Tenkai Stone**, and it's on Earth. She sasses him
to his face ("All this time and you still haven't found the Tenkai Fortress? That's kind
of lame") and accepts with mock servility: "You bet. As Vilius commands." Then, alone, the
truth: the Stone is the "shiny bling thing" her dad left her in a fancy gift box — "I knew
it must be important. But THIS important? … So Vilius thinks this will help him find the
Fortress, huh? Not if I happen to find it first." She serves no one; she runs both sides.
(The Stone projects images/light when activated; she can't fully control it — "Anyone in
there speak English? Piece of junk!")

<!--ep:34-->
Same episode, on Earth: she tells Guren which side she's on and lectures him and Ceylan on
why it's best to work alone. On Quarton she nearly finishes Bravenwolf — Ceylan unlocks
Elemental Titan Mode to save him. She escapes and gives Vilius the Fortress's location,
buying herself free rein as long as the Fortress is found.

<!--ep:36-->
Granox and Slyger locate the Fortress and squabble over credit; Vilius zaps them both.

<!--ep:37-->
The Knights find the Fortress sealed behind an impenetrable barrier. (On Earth this is the
episode a five-year-old named **Kiiro** — Chooki's little cousin with a stuffed dog named
Sandy — first appears, babysat by the boys. Beni is NOT part of that story yet; the
encounter that matters comes at ep 46.)

<!--ep:38-->
Open war for the Fortress ("Dragon²" battle). Vilius: whoever controls the Fortress
controls both worlds. The arc ends with Beni positioned exactly where she likes it: paid by
Vilius, loyal to no one, holding the Stone's secret in her pocket.

## Continuity anchors

<!--ep:38-->
- Gen and Beni are SPLIT for this whole arc — do not write them as partners here.
- Eurus is corrupted/enslaved by Vilius from ep 27 (her old mentor, lost).
- The Tenkai Stone: her father's gift, key to the Fortress, hers — the single most
  important object she owns, and nobody knows she has it until she chooses.
