## The world's ground rules

<!--ep:1-->
Two worlds exist. **Earth** — ordinary, sunny Benham City. **Quarton** — a planet of living
blocks, torn by endless war over **Tenkai Energy**, the resource everything there runs on.
The wars were driven by the evil Tenkai Knight **Vilius**; opposing him stand the **Corekai**
(good army, led in the field by Commander **Beag**) against Vilius's **Corrupted**. Four
Guardians (Boreas chief among them) watch over Quarton and choose champions. A hidden
**interdimensional portal** links the worlds; its Earth side sits inside **Mr. White's Shop
of Wonders**, an antiques shop Mr. White keeps. Earth boys who hold a **core brick** can
transfer to Quarton and materialize there as legendary Tenkai Knights — their bodies
shape-shift into block-built armored forms; on Earth they're just kids.

<!--ep:1-->
Guren Nash moves to Benham City, meets Ceylan, and both are pulled to Quarton for the first
time. Guren becomes **Bravenwolf** (red, fire), Ceylan **Tributon** (blue, ice). The four
Knights of legend once sealed Vilius; the Guardians have summoned successors.

<!--ep:3-->
The other two are found already active: Chooki Mason (**Lydendor**, yellow, lightning — the
school's effortless star athlete) and Toxsa Dalton (**Valorn**, green, nature/tech — gamer,
youngest, his family runs a diner the group hangs out at). Friendship starts bumpy; they
become a team over these episodes.

## The Dragon Cube quest (the arc's engine)

<!--ep:2-->
Quarton's history: the **Tenkai Dragon**, the mightiest force, was split into **five Dragon
Cubes** scattered across Quarton. Whoever reunites them and opens them with the **Dragon
Key** awakens the Dragon and decides both worlds' fate. Both sides race for the cubes.

<!--ep:5-->
Vilius reveals himself in force once the boys grow confident. The Guardians warn against
overconfidence. Across the arc the boys win cubes one by one (Granox and Slyger — Vilius's
two bickering lieutenants — keep failing him).

<!--ep:7-->
A mysterious transfer student, **Gen Inukai**, appears in Benham City; on Quarton a black
doppelganger of Bravenwolf, **Dromus**, debuts. (They are the same person, revealed later.)
Titan Mode: each Knight can power up into a giant Tenkai Titan form; unlocking it is
personal — Guren's fire, Chooki overcoming a jinx (ep 8), Toxsa learning he needs the team
(ep 10).

<!--ep:9-->
Commander Beag sacrifices himself to save the Knights, unlocking their first **Robofusion**
(Knights combining into one larger machine); he survives, rescued from the Valley of
Secrets, where the fifth cube is found.

<!--ep:11-->
Dromus wields a **Core Corrupter** that can turn Corekai evil; the Knights reverse it. Gen,
on Earth, needles Guren with talk about "choosing sides."

<!--ep:12-->
The **Dragon Key** is found (Guren carries it); Gen shadows the boys.

<!--ep:13-->
Endgame of the arc: the five cubes combine; the Key awakens the **Tenkai Dragon** — but
Dromus springs a trap and the Dragon is captured, transported to the Corrupted headquarters.
In the battle Guren reveals his human identity to Dromus. Cliffhanger: the Dragon is in
Vilius's hands and the Key is lost.

## What matters for later

<!--ep:13-->
- The Key being **lost** is why the next arc hunts the **Black Dragon Key** (its dark twin).
- Gen/Dromus is established as a lone operator with his own agenda — the partner Beni
  arrives to join in ep 14.
- Beni does not exist in this arc: no character has met her, and she has met none of them
  (she has, however, already been to Quarton in her past, trained by Dromus — she just has
  no contact with the four boys yet).
