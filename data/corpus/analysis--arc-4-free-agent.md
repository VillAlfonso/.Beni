## The Beast World detour (eps 39–43)

<!--ep:39-->
Mid-battle, the Knights, Vilius's forces, Beag AND Venetta are all yanked into the **Beast
World** — a nonsense-rules pocket world run by beast lords (the monkey king **Orangor**,
and above him **Scorpidon**) who trap visitors and make them play games for their freedom.
Nobody can portal home until the hosts are entertained.

<!--ep:40-->
In a cave the boys and Beni are handed their Quarton weapons in human form; the beasts run
them through game after game (a robofusion race among them — Venetta robofuses too). The
episodes run on comedy: promised wishes never paid out, "one last game" that never is.

<!--ep:41-->
**The princess and the wish — the arc's real Beni moment.** In the Beast World's games she
gets crowned the princess of the nonsense kingdom, and she WINS the great race — earning a
wish from the beasts. She wishes for **all of them to go home**. The beasts answer: the
wish covers only her — she alone can leave. And Beni — the friendless girl, the "flying
solo is the only way to go" girl — refuses to go home alone. She's visibly sad about it,
then trades her wish away for *another game*, keeping everyone stuck together rather than
leaving the boys behind. Nobody makes a speech about it. It says everything about who's
actually under the armor, three arcs before she'd admit it.

<!--ep:43-->
Scorpidon's actual motive: he just wanted to be entertained. Satisfied, he frees them; he
gives Guren a parting gift — a **wolf's-head emblem** (this matters at the finale). The
stretch matters for Beni mostly for what it forced: stuck in the same boat, she cooperated
with the Knights for weeks, and they all know it — "we would never have gotten out of the
Beast World without your help" (Guren). Her spin: "That's only because I wanted to get out
of that dump as much as anyone."

## Home, and the barbecue (eps 44–45)

<!--ep:44-->
Back on Quarton the fortress standoff resumes; the Knights come home rusty. Beni watches
both sides fight from a distance like it's television: "Every time it looks like those four
sappy do-gooders are finally gonna get it, they luck out. It's like a bad soap opera… And
that goes for Vilius, too. Trying to prove how macho they are by fighting over the Tenkai
Fortress, when THIS is all they need. What good's the Fortress without the key?" — she
holds the Stone and savors being the only one who understands the board.

<!--ep:44-->
Gen — now orbiting the Knights' side — confronts her twice: "Mess with the Tenkai Knights
and you'll have to deal with me." Her deflections: "You always this paranoid, or is it just
in the morning?" and "I've gotta tell the zoo one of their cuckoos is missing."

<!--ep:44-->
**The barbecue.** The boys throw a relaxation barbecue (Mr. White: "barbecue master, third
degree"). Guren runs into Beni in town and invites her; she refuses until he describes the
menu ("There's nothing like buttery corn on the cob off the grill… All right, you win").
She attends — an enemy at their party — sniping at Gen ("I was invited. Were you?").
Rain kills the grill. Guren explains he planned it thinking of his dad: "it's not about the
food, it's about the way it brings people together." Beni: "Cue the violins. What a sappy
story. I'm outta here." Gen, watching her go: **"Seems like Guren's story struck a nerve."**
(It did: fathers, and the family warmth she never got.) Later in the episode Dromus unlocks
Elemental Mode through his bond with Bravenwolf — Gen's redemption is outpacing hers, and
she attends the party of people she officially still fights.

<!--ep:45-->
"The Four Beags": fortress defense continues; the Guardians scheme about Beag; on Earth a
new teacher arrives at the boys' school. Beni-light breather before her turn.

## Continuity anchors

<!--ep:45-->
- Beast World cooperation is canon shared history between Beni and all four boys.
- Guren's wolf's-head emblem from Scorpidon foreshadows the Tenkai Wolf (finale).
- Beni still holds the Tenkai Stone; Vilius still believes she serves him. Both clocks
  are ticking.
