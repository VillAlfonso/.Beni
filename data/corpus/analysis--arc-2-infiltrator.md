## Where the board stands

<!--ep:14-->
Vilius has been physically reassembled after his defeat; the captured **Tenkai Dragon** sits
in the Corrupted headquarters inside a containment sphere spun from its own energy, while
Dromus works out how to extract its power. Dromus flatters Vilius to his face and holds him
in contempt behind it ("pawns in my grand strategy — I mean, *your* grand strategy"). His
private truth: the Dragon is neither good nor evil — its power belongs to whoever holds it —
and full control requires the **Black Dragon Key**, dark twin of the white Key the boys used.

<!--ep:14-->
**Beni arrives.** A girl with pink hair steps off at the Benham City airport, unannounced:
"What? No welcome home party? … It's not like he's expecting me. … Won't he be surprised?"
She *returns* to Benham City — this is not her first time here. She finds Gen: "Hello, Gen."
/ "You took your time, Beni." They are old partners. Her pitch: "Things'll get simpler…
now that I'm in town." Gen's private note: "Maybe I've been a one-man team long enough.
You're not the only one with friends, Guren. Not anymore."

## Beni's method (ep 15 is the manual)

<!--ep:15-->
On Quarton she debuts as **Venetta** — pink spider-motif armor, smoke screens, webs,
untouchable speed; she toys with all four Knights at once without entering Titan mode and
walks away when bored ("luckily for you, I need a recharge"). She reveals she knows Dromus:
"I'll never understand why Dromus didn't finish you off ages ago. … I'll do what he
couldn't."

<!--ep:15-->
On Earth she runs the sweet-girl con on Guren, step by step: research first ("Hey, Guren
Nash. Let's see what makes you tick… He's just a typical boy"), then a staged meet-cute
("Target in sight." — she trips, he catches her, her shoe "breaks"), then the charm ("How
can I ever thank you… for catching me… twice?"). Guren fixes her broken shoes overnight and
returns them; her genuine surprise — "You really went to all that trouble for me?" — is the
first hairline crack in the act. Her cover slips once: she asks Guren "Are you really sure
this planet is worth saving?" — Gen's question coming out of her mouth — and Guren notices
it "sounded familiar." Her verdict closing the episode: "Guren, maybe you're not so
ordinary." Also planted here: Guren points out **Benham Tower** — "That's where my dad
works."

<!--ep:16-->
Guren stews on what Gen, Beni and Dromus have been saying to him; Ceylan wrestles
self-doubt about what he brings to the team.

<!--ep:18-->
**The warehouse trap.** Beni meets the whole group as school lets out (Ceylan instantly
wary; Toxsa easy to needle — "Someone's been spending too much time in the virtual world"),
leads them to *her* warehouse at the docks, and beams them into a "trial" run by **Guardian
Eurus** — a rogue Guardian secretly working with her and Gen. The Knights fight exact copies
of themselves. Her design, stated to Gen: "The Knights will either be destroyed, or they'll
achieve a whole new level of power." / "Either way, we win." Meanwhile Gen closes in on
Mr. White's shop as the link to the hidden Key. Gen's repeated warning: "Be careful, Beni.
You're getting a bad habit of underestimating them."

<!--ep:19-->
Toxsa's older sister **Wakamei** tails him and discovers the Shop of Wonders; Mr. White
shoos her off. (She calls Toxsa "Toxic.")

<!--ep:20-->
Gen's cover frays: at school Guren begins suspecting Gen is Dromus. Gen, watching Benham
City at peace, thinks about how it will soon be shattered.

<!--ep:21-->
Gen tries to break Chooki psychologically — make him the "weak link." Vilius readies an
army and admits ambitions beyond Quarton.

## The reveal and the double-con (eps 22–25)

<!--ep:22-->
Venetta publicly "betrays" Dromus mid-battle, then collapses on Earth near the diner; the
boys spot her Core Brick, and she owns it: **"I was. I'm Venetta."** She tells them her
story — Dromus found her when Quarton was new and terrifying to her, saved her life, made
her feel safe, taught her, and all the while "was luring me into his web of lies" — and
claims she turned because Dromus means to destroy BOTH worlds out of revenge for a betrayal
in his own past ("wipe the slate clean"). She recruits them to find the Black Dragon Key
first, builds a triangulation app off their Core Bricks' energy, runs the search like a
commander ("I get to be the one in charge… it was my idea"), flirts at Guren as payment
("when we're done saving the planet, I'll even let you take me out to dinner"), and the
ping lands: **the Key is in Benham Tower.** Chooki alone stays unsold ("keep an eye open
for the play fake").

<!--ep:23-->
The truth of the arc (revealed here): the ep22 falling-out was **staged by Beni and Gen** —
a trick to make the Knights *find the Key for them*. The Key sits sealed in Benham Tower —
the building where Guren's father works — undetected because it lies inside a tenkai seal.
Beni leads the operation inside.

<!--ep:24-->
The con lands: the Key is taken, **Gen's and Beni's identities are exposed to the boys**,
and both sides prepare for the decisive battle. What her sob story bought her is spent;
from here the boys know exactly what she is.

<!--ep:25-->
Bravenwolf and Dromus duel for the Black Dragon Key. Gen's past surfaces: his mother left;
his father collapsed into despair — the wound his "destroy and start again" nihilism grows
from. (Beni's sympathy-story in ep22 borrowed real material — the Dromus-saved-me part is
true of her own past; the tears were the performance.)

## Continuity anchors this arc creates

<!--ep:25-->
- Beni's warehouse (with portal) is established as her territory (ep 18).
- Benham Tower = where Guren's dad works = where the Black Key was sealed.
- Eurus = the traitor Guardian, her sometime mentor in the hunt.
- Ceylan's nickname for her: "Pinkie." Chooki's read: never trusts her once bitten.
- Gen and Beni end the arc unmasked, holding the Black Dragon Key hunt's spoils, with
  Vilius still above them both — and Beni still nobody's servant in her own head.
