Five weeks after the American premiere, Tenkai Knights started airing in Canada. It was broadcasted on Teletoon, a children's broadcaster who also is associated with Corus Entertainment. In the past, Teletoon already has broadcasted several notable anime such as Spider Riders and Bakugan. Canada is the home market of Spin Master who serves as the co-producer of Tenkai Knights. So along with Japan and the USA, it is the most important market. With Tenkai Knights' success in Canada, there could be the clincher for a second season.

## Story

Long ago, the peaceful, cube-shaped planet Quarton was torn by war between the Corekai and the Corrupted, led by the evil Villius. Only the Tenkai Knights - Bravenwolf, Tributon, Valorn and Lydendor - were strong enough to defeat Villius and his forces. After the warlord's defeat, neither Villius nor the Tenkai Knights were seen again.

Now on planet Earth in the year 2034, Villius has returned and plots to take control of both Quarton and Earth. Four human boys - Guren, Ceylan, Toxsa, and Chooki - are chosen to be the new Tenkai Knights to defend both Earth and Quarton.

## Trivia

- Canada was the second country to start Tenkai Knights just after the United States and the first to finish it.
- With 338 days, Tenkai Knights had the third longest runtime in Canada, just behind the USA (406) and Japan (358).
- 34 episodes had their global premiere in Canada (17-38, 41-52), more than the USA (16) and Australia (2).
