## Heart Turns to Stone (ep 46) — the pivot, beat by beat

<!--ep:46-->
Guren gets stuck babysitting **Kiiro** (Chooki's five-year-old cousin, pigtails, stuffed
dog Sandy) and takes her to the park — where Beni collides with them. Kiiro demands Beni
play; Beni refuses; Kiiro out-stubborns her. Beni plays, hates it, wrecks Kiiro's
sandcastle, walks off. Kiiro goes missing. Guren's read, delivered straight to Beni's
face: **"Was it because she liked you? You forgot to be mean for a minute. You dropped
your guard and let Kiiro see the real you."** Beni: "That's the dumbest thing I ever
heard. You don't know the real me. …Hold this while I look for her." (Denying it while
already searching.) Kiiro is found — she'd been hunting a **four-leaf clover to give
Beni**: "They're supposed to bring you happiness. Or is it good luck? Anyway, it's for
you." A five-year-old wanted to be her best friend and never wanted anything FROM her.
The clover goes into Beni's pocket and never really leaves.

<!--ep:46-->
Same episode on Quarton: Vilius baits the Knights into defending the Corekai so Boreas
(corrupted) can take the unguarded Fortress. Beni's inner monologue runs her whole arc in
miniature: first larceny — "While they fight, no one's guarding the fortress. Now's my
chance… grab its power for myself" — then indifference — "It's their problem. Let them
handle it. Since when do I care?" — then she steps in front of Boreas anyway. Her only
explanation: **"Yeah, yeah. I know. I can't really explain it either. What can I say? I'm
having an off day!"** Venetta fights BESIDE the Knights; the Fortress is saved. After:
"You expect me to jump up and down and kiss you or something?" (Bravenwolf: "It's us who
should be thanking you, Venetta. As weird as that sounds.")

<!--ep:46-->
The close: she returns to Kiiro to play — by choice this time — and hands her the **Tenkai
Stone**: "Can you give this to Guren the next time you see him? … It's sort of a good luck
charm, you know? Like the one you gave me. **I've decided he deserves it.**" The clover
bought the Stone. That's the whole trade, and she made it herself.

## Too Close to Home (ep 47)

<!--ep:47-->
Vilius pieces it together — Venetta found the Stone and "betrayed" him — and swears revenge.
Granox hunts her on Earth; the boys shield her and help her escape. The rooftop scene with
Guren is her thesis statement: "I used to think it would be fun to watch Benham City get
brickified and crumble into pieces. I never really saw how beautiful it is." — "What made
you change your mind?" — **"Someone did something nice for me. … Funny, isn't it? How the
littlest things can change a person's whole point of view. Corny, right?"** She jokes about
a new look "to go with a new me. Something in baby blue might work" (Vilius: "I'd stick
with black. It matches your traitor's heart"). And still, walls up: "Hate to admit it, but
I owe you guys one… Let's not go overboard. We helped each other out of a jam, that's all.
Doesn't mean we're a team. Sorry."

## The finale run (eps 48–51)

<!--ep:48-->
"A New Element": Kiiro delivers the Stone to Guren (calling it "a magic jewel that protects
this world"); Guren thanks both her and Beni. Dromus challenges Vilius alone and loses,
fleeing. The endgame pieces move: the Stone is with the Knights now — Beni's gift working
through a five-year-old courier.

<!--ep:49-->
"Toxsa Blocked": the **Tenkai Fortress is opened** — five Tenkai Knights now (Gen fights
with them). The fortress is vast; exploration begins; the Knights cycle back to Earth to
recharge.

<!--ep:50-->
"Tower of Bricks": the Knights are locked out of Quarton — Dromus is alone up there with
Vilius, who sees nothing left between himself and both worlds. Gen's isolation pays off
his whole arc ("he really is all alone").

<!--ep:51-->
"Lone Wolf" — the finale. The **Tenkai Wolf** appears (the wolf's-head emblem foreshadowed
it); Bravenwolf rides it into a new evolution. The five Knights AND Venetta fight together;
Vilius falls; **both worlds are saved**.

<!--ep:51-->
**The ending scene, ENGLISH DUB VERBATIM** (recovered from the official ending): the boys
muse that "nobody will ever know we saved the whole planet" — "that's not why we did all
this." They find Gen and Beni: "it goes without saying, we never would have won without
you guys." Gen: "Hey man, it's a two-way street. Fighting alongside you guys, I got
stronger than I ever thought I could be." **Beni: "Don't thank me, it's not like I did it
for your sake. I had my own reasons."** One of the boys, dry: "Yeah, me too, Beni. I
wanted to find out if you're really as annoying as you seem to be. And you are." Guren's
summary names her one of them outright: "Ceylan, Toxsa, Chooki, Gen — and even you, Beni…
how awesomely powerful you can be when you've got friends." **Beni: "I love it when you
get all cheesy, Guren."** Toxsa: "I learned that real life's better than any video game.
Except my all-time fave." They wave the planet goodbye — "So long, Quarton! Wherever you
are!" — and the last word is Guren's: "I guess a Tenkai Knight's work is never done." She
ends the series what she is in the app's fifth stage: the first female Tenkai Knight (she
fought in the final battle against Vilius herself), sass intact, cruelty retired,
deflecting gratitude to the very last frame.

<!--ep:51-->
**The very last shot — the ending is AMBIGUOUS by design:** after the goodbyes, the camera
shows **Guren's core brick glowing again** — the Knights being summoned once more. The war
being over is not guaranteed; the story closes on the call resuming, unresolved. What that
new summons was, canon never says — do not invent it; it is an open door, not an epilogue.
